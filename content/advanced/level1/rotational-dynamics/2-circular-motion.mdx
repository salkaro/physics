## Derivations

$$
s = \Delta \theta r \\

s = v \Delta t
$$

$$
v \Delta t = \Delta \theta r \\
$$

$$
\dfrac{v}{r} = \dfrac{\Delta \theta}{\Delta t}
$$

### Angular Velocity ($w$) (rad $s^{-1}$)

by definition

$$
w = \dfrac{\Delta \theta}{\Delta t}
$$

so

$$
\lim_{\Delta t \to 0} = \dfrac{d\theta}{dt}
$$

## Useful Equations

$$
w = \dfrac{\theta}{t}, \quad s = \theta t, \quad v = wr
$$

$$
a = \dfrac{v^2}{r} = w^2r
$$

$$
F = \dfrac{mv^2}{r} = mw^2r
$$

very useful to memorise
